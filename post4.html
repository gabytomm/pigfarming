1. Lack of Planning
Jumping into pig farming without a clear business plan or feasibility study is a recipe for disaster. Before starting, you must identify your goals, budget, target market, and long-term strategy. Understand local demand, supply chains, and laws governing livestock farming in your area.

2. Choosing the Wrong Breed
Not all pigs are created equal. Some are best for meat, others for breeding. New farmers often select pigs based on cost rather than suitability. Choose a breed that matches your climate, farming system, and business goals. Consult local experts or successful farmers for advice.

3. Poor Housing Conditions
Inadequate shelter leads to stress, disease, and low productivity. Pens should be well-ventilated, spacious, and easy to clean. Failing to plan for drainage and sanitation can turn your pigsty into a breeding ground for infections.
